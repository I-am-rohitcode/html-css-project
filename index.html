About

Kevin Feige helped conceive of a shared media universe of Marvel properties.

Marvel Entertainment began producing its own films independently in 2005 and distributed them through Paramount Pictures. In 2007, Marvel Studios raised funding from a $525 million revolving credit facility with Merrill Lynch. The plan was to release individual films for their main characters and then merge them in a crossover film. In November 2013, Marvel Studios president Kevin Feige stated that "in an ideal world" releases each year would include one film based on an existing character and one featuring a new character, saying it is "certainly something to aim for."

Feige expanded on this in July 2014, stating that the studio was not "a number cruncher" telling the studio to increase their film output but rather to manage existing franchises. After the titles were revealed in October 2014, Feige said that the studio was firing on all cylinders right now, which made them comfortable for the first time... to increase to three films a year [in 2017 and 2018] instead of just two, without changing their methods.

In February 2014, Feige stated that Marvel Studios wants to mimic the "rhythm" that the comic books have developed by having the characters appear in their own films and then come together, much like a big event or crossover series. Avengers films acting as "big, giant linchpins" are seen as a way to teach the general movie-going audience about the notion of the characters existing separately, coming together for specific events, and going away and existing separately in their own worlds again.

In April 2016, Feige announced that the universe will move to Phase Four and reflect on the first three phases, with Phase Three being the "deconstruction Phase" of the MCU.

Phases 1

Iron Man

Iron Man is a 2008 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character. Produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Paramount Pictures, it is the first film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). Directed by Jon Favreau, the film stars Robert Downey Jr. as Tony Stark / Iron Man, with other notable actors including Terrence Howard, Jeff Bridges, Gwyneth Paltrow, Leslie Bibb, and Shaun Toub. Stark, a renowned industrialist and engineer, builds a mechanized armor suit and becomes the superhero.

The Incredible Hulk

The Incredible Hulk is a 2008 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character Hulk. Produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Universal Pictures, it is the second film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Directed by Louis Leterrier, the film stars Edward Norton as Bruce Banner and stars Liv Tyler, Tim Roth, William Hurt, Tim Blake Nelson, Ty Burrell, and Christina Cabot. Banner becomes the Hulk as part of a military scheme to revive the "Super-Soldier" program.

Iron Man 2

Iron Man 2 is a 2010 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character Iron Man. Produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Paramount Pictures, it is the sequel to Iron Man (2008) and the third film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Directed by Jon Favreau and written by Justin Theroux, the film stars Robert Downey Jr. as Tony Stark, Gwyneth Paltrow, Don Cheadle, Scarlett Johansson, Sam Rockwell, Mickey Rourke, and Samuel L. Jackson. The film explores Tony Stark's declining health and the Russian scientist Ivan Vanko's vendetta against the Stark family.

Thor

Thor is a 2011 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character. Produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Paramount Pictures, it is the fourth film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Directed by Kenneth Branagh, the film stars Chris Hemsworth, Natalie Portman, Tom Hiddleston, Stellan Skarsgård, and others. After a dormant war, Thor is banished from Asgard to Earth and stripped of his powers. As his brother Loki plots to take the Asgardian throne, Thor must prove himself worthy.

Captain America: The First Avenger

Captain America: The First Avenger is a 2011 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character Captain America. Produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Paramount Pictures, it is the fifth film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Directed by Joe Johnston, the film stars Chris Evans as Captain America, alongside Tommy Lee Jones, Hugo Weaving, Hayley Atwell, Sebastian Stan, Dominic Cooper, Toby Jones, Neal McDonough, Derek Luke, and Stanley Tucci. The film tells the story of Captain America's transformation during World War II.

The Avengers

Marvel's The Avengers is a 2012 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics superhero team. Produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures, it is the sixth film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). Directed by Joss Whedon, the film stars an ensemble cast including Robert Downey Jr., Chris Evans, Mark Ruffalo, Chris Hemsworth, Scarlett Johansson, and Jeremy Renner. The Avengers, along with Nick Fury and S.H.I.E.L.D., recruit Tony Stark, Steve Rogers, Bruce Banner, Thor, Natasha Romanoff, and Clint Barton to stop Thor's brother Loki from subjugating Earth.

Phases 2

Iron Man 3

Iron Man 3 is a 2013 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character Iron Man, produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. It is the sequel to Iron Man (2008) and Iron Man 2 (2010) and the seventh film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). Directed by Shane Black, the film stars Robert Downey Jr. as Tony Stark / Iron Man, alongside Gwyneth Paltrow, Don Cheadle, Guy Pearce, Rebecca Hall, Stéphanie Szostak, James Badge Dale, William Sadler, Miguel Ferrer, Jon Favreau, and Ben Kingsley.

Thor: The Dark World

Thor: The Dark World is a 2013 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character Thor, directed by Alan Taylor and starring Chris Hemsworth as Thor, Natalie Portman, Tom Hiddleston, Stellan Skarsgård, Idris Elba, Christopher Eccleston, Adewale Akinnuoye-Agbaje, Kat Dennings, Ray Stevenson, Zachary Levi, Tadanobu Asano, Jaimie Alexander, Rene Russo, and Anthony Hopkins. The film follows Thor and Loki (Hiddleston) as they team up to save the Nine Realms from the Dark Elves.

Captain America: The Winter Soldier

Captain America: The Winter Soldier is a 2014 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character Captain America. Produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures, it is the sequel to Captain America: The First Avenger (2011) and the ninth film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). Directed by Anthony and Joe Russo, the film stars Chris Evans as Captain America, Scarlett Johansson as Scarlett Johansson, and Sebastian Stan as Falcon. Captain America teams up with Black Widow and Falcon to uncover a conspiracy within S.H.I.E.L.D. while facing the Winter Soldier.

Guardians of the Galaxy

Guardians of the Galaxy is a 2014 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics superhero team. Produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures, it is the 10th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). Directed by James Gunn, the film stars Chris Pratt, Zoe Saldaña, Dave Bautista, Vin Diesel, and Bradley Cooper as the Guardians. The film follows Peter Quill and a group of extraterrestrial criminals on the run after stealing a powerful artifact.

Avengers: Age of Ultron

Avengers: Age of Ultron is a 2015 American superhero film, the sequel to The Avengers (2012) and the 11th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Directed by Joss Whedon, the film stars an ensemble cast including Robert Downey Jr., Chris Hemsworth, Mark Ruffalo, Chris Evans, Scarlett Johansson, Jeremy Renner, Don Cheadle, Aaron Taylor-Johnson, Elizabeth Olsen, Paul Bettany, Cobie Smulders, Anthony Mackie, Hayley Atwell, Idris Elba, Linda Cardellini, Stellan Skarsgård, James Spader, and Samuel L. Jackson. The Avengers fight Ultron, an artificial intelligence created by Tony Stark and Bruce Banner, who plans to cause human extinction for world peace.

Ant-Man

Ant-Man is a 2015 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics characters Scott Lang and Hank Pym. Produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures, it is the 12th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). Directed by Peyton Reed, the film stars Rudd as Scott Lang / Ant-Man, Evangeline Lilly, Corey Stoll, Bobby Cannavale, Michael Peña, Tip "T.I." Harris, Anthony Mackie, Wood Harris, Judy Greer, Abby Ryder Fortson, David Dastmalchian, and Michael Douglas as Hank Pym.

Phases 3

Captain America: Civil War

Captain America: Civil War is a 2016 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character Captain America. Produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures, it is the 13th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). Directed by Anthony and Joe Russo, the film stars Chris Evans as Captain America and stars an ensemble cast including Robert Downey Jr., Scarlett Johansson, and others. The film's plot revolves around a disagreement over international oversight of the Avengers, dividing the team into two opposing factions: one led by Steve Rogers and the other by Tony Stark.

Doctor Strange

Doctor Strange is a 2016 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character. Produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures, it is the 14th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Directed by Scott Derrickson, the film stars Benedict Cumberbatch as neurosurgeon Stephen Strange, with other notable actors. Strange learns the mystic arts after a career-ending car crash.

Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 2

Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 2 is a 2017 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics superhero team. Directed by James Gunn, the film stars an ensemble cast including Chris Pratt, Zoe Saldaña, Dave Bautista, Vin Diesel, Bradley Cooper, Michael Rooker, Karen Gillan, Pom Klementieff, Sylvester Stallone, and Kurt Russell. The Guardians travel through the cosmos to help Peter Quill learn more about his mysterious parentage. The film was officially announced at the 2014 San Diego Comic-Con International and was directed by James Gunn.

Spider-Man: Homecoming

Spider-Man: Homecoming is a 2017 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character Spider-Man, co-produced by Columbia Pictures and Marvel Studios. It is the second Spider-Man film reboot and the 16th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Directed by Jon Watts, the film stars Tom Holland as Peter Parker, alongside Michael Keaton, Jon Favreau, Gwyneth Paltrow, Zendaya, Donald Glover, Jacob Batalon, Laura Harrier, Tony Revolori, Bokeem Woodbine, Tyne Daly, Marisa Tomei, and Robert Downey Jr. The film explores Peter Parker's struggle to balance high school life with being Spider-Man.

Thor: Ragnarok

Thor: Ragnarok is a 2017 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character Thor, directed by Taika Waititi and produced by Marvel Studios and Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. It is the 17th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) and follows Thor's quest to escape the alien planet Sakaar to save Asgard from Hela (Blanchett) and the impending Ragnarök. The film stars Chris Hemsworth as Thor and stars Tom Hiddleston, Cate Blanchett, Idris Elba, Jeff Goldblum, Tessa Thompson, Karl Urban, Mark Ruffalo, and Anthony Hopkins.

Black Panther

Black Panther is a 2018 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character. Directed by Ryan Coogler and co-written by Joe Robert Cole, it stars Chadwick Boseman as T'Challa / Black Panther, Michael B. Jordan as Wakanda's king, and a diverse cast. The film follows T'Challa's quest to challenge Killmonger, who plans to abandon Wakanda's isolationist policies and initiate a global revolution. The film is the 18th in the Marvel Cinematic Universe.

Avengers: Infinity War

Avengers: Infinity War is a 2018 American superhero film directed by Anthony and Joe Russo and written by Christopher Markus and Stephen McFeely. It follows the Avengers and Guardians of the Galaxy as they fight Thanos to collect the six Infinity Stones, which are used to kill half of all life in the universe. The film stars an ensemble cast including Robert Downey Jr., Chris Hemsworth, Mark Ruffalo, Chris Evans, Scarlett Johansson, Benedict Cumberbatch, Don Cheadle, Tom Holland, Chadwick Boseman, Paul Bettany, Elizabeth Olsen, Anthony Mackie, Sebastian Stan, Danai Gurira, Letitia Wright, Dave Bautista, Zoe Saldaña, Josh Brolin, and Chris Pratt.

Ant-Man and the Wasp

Ant-Man and the Wasp is a 2018 American superhero film based on Marvel Comics, featuring Scott Lang / Ant-Man and Hope Pym / Wasp. Produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures, it is the 20th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). Directed by Peyton Reed, the film was written by Chris McKenna, Erik Sommers, Paul Rudd, Andrew Barrer, and Gabriel Ferrari. The film stars Rudd as Lang and Evangeline Lilly as Hope van Dyne, with other notable actors. The titular characters work with Hank Pym to retrieve Janet van Dyne from the Quantum Realm.

Captain Marvel

Captain Marvel is a 2019 American superhero film based on Marvel Comics, directed by Anna Boden and Ryan Fleck, and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. Avengers: Endgame

Avengers: Endgame is a 2019 American superhero film, the direct sequel to Avengers: Infinity War, and the 22nd film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures, the film stars an ensemble cast including Robert Downey Jr., Chris Evans, Mark Ruffalo, Chris Hemsworth, Scarlett Johansson, Jeremy Renner, Don Cheadle, Paul Rudd, Brie Larson, Karen Gillan, Danai Gurira, Benedict Wong, Jon Favreau, Bradley Cooper, Gwyneth Paltrow, and Josh Brolin.

Phases 4

Black Widow

Black Widow is a 2021 American superhero film based on Marvel Comics, directed by Cate Shortland and starring Scarlett Johansson as Natasha Romanoff. Produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures, it is the 24th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Set after Captain America: Civil War 2016, the film follows Romanoff on the run and confronts her past as a Russian spy before becoming an Avenger.

Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings

Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings is a 2021 American superhero film based on Marvel Comics, directed by Destin Daniel Cretton and starring Simu Liu. The 25th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe, it follows Shang-Chi as he confronts his past when his father, Wenwu, the leader of the Ten Rings terrorist organization, forces him and his sister Xialing to search for a mythical village. The film was produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures.

Eternals

Eternals is a 2021 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics race. Produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures, it is the 26th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Directed by Chloé Zhao, the film stars an ensemble cast including Gemma Chan, Richard Madden, Kumail Nanjiani, Lia McHugh, Brian Tyree Henry, Lauren Ridloff, Barry Keoghan, Don Lee, Kit Harington, Salma Hayek, and Angelina Jolie. The Eternals, immortal alien beings, emerge from hiding to protect Earth from their ancient counterparts, the Deviants.

Spider-Man: No Way Home

Spider-Man: No Way Home is a 2021 American superhero film, co-produced by Columbia Pictures and Marvel Studios, and distributed by Sony Pictures Releasing. It is the sequel to Spider-Man: Homecoming (2017) and Spider-Man: Far From Home (2019), and the 27th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). Directed by Jon Watts and written by Chris McKenna and Erik Sommers, the film stars Tom Holland as Peter Parker, Benedict Cumberbatch, and other notable actors. Parker asks Dr. Stephen Strange to use magic to keep his identity a secret, but when the spell goes wrong, the multiverse is opened, allowing visitors from alternate realities to enter his universe.

Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness

Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness is a 2022 American superhero film, directed by Sam Raimi and written by Michael Waldron, and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. The film stars Benedict Cumberbatch as Doctor Strange, Elizabeth Olsen as Wanda Maximoff, and Benedict Wong as America Chavez. The film is the 28th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU).

Thor: Love and Thunder

Thor: Love and Thunder is a 2022 American superhero film based on Marvel Comics, directed by Taika Waititi and co-written by Jennifer Kaytin Robinson. It is the 29th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe and follows Thor as he tries to find inner peace. The film stars Chris Hemsworth as Thor, Christian Bale, Tessa Thompson, Jaimie Alexander, Russell Crowe, and Natalie Portman. Thor must recruit Valkyrie, Korg, and Jane Foster to stop Gorr the God Butcher from eliminating all gods.

Black Panther: Wakanda Forever

Black Panther: Wakanda Forever is a 2022 American superhero film based on Marvel Comics, directed by Ryan Coogler and co-written by Joe Robert Cole. It is the 30th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe and follows the leaders of Wakanda as they fight to protect their nation after King T'Challa's death. The film stars Letitia Wright as Shuri / Black Panther, Lupita Nyong'o, Danai Gurira, Winston Duke, Florence Kasumba, Dominique Thorne, Michaela Coel, Mabel Cadena, Tenoch Huerta Mejía, Martin Freeman, Julia Louis-Dreyfus, and Angela Bassett.

The Marvels

The Marvels is a 2023 American superhero film, directed by Nia DaCosta and co-written by Megan McDonnell and Elissa Karasik. Fantastic Four(2025)

The Marvel Comics superhero team, Fantastic Four, has been featured in four live-action films since its inception. Constantin Film acquired the rights in 1986, followed by a low-budget film in 1992 and a sequel in 2007. Both films earned a combined US$635 million worldwide. However, 20th Century Fox's disappointment with the box-office return of Fantastic Four: Rise of the Silver Surfer led to the cancellation of a potential third film and a Silver Surfer spin-off.

Thunderbolts (2025)

A Thunderbolts film is in development, directed by Jake Schreier and written by Eric Pearson. The team includes Sebastian Stan as Bucky Barnes, Hannah John-Kamen as Ava Starr, Wyatt Russell as John Walker, Julia Louis-Dreyfus as Valentina Allegra de Fontaine, Florence Pugh as Yelena Belova, David Harbour as Alexei Shostakov, and Olga Kurylenko as Antonia Dreykov. Lee Sung Jin is set to rewrite the script by March 2023. Filming is expected to begin in Atlanta, Georgia, and Utah in March or April 2024, with the film set to release on July 25, 2025.

Blade (2025)

Marvel Studios had a working script for a new Blade film by May 2013, after regaining rights after New Line Cinema's previous film series. Mahershala Ali approached Marvel Studios about starring in the film, having previously portrayed Cornell "Cottonmouth" Stokes in Marvel Television's Luke Cage. Feige officially announced the film with Ali in the title role in July. Stacy Osei-Kuffour was hired to write the film, with Bassam Tariq hired to direct in September. Yann Demange was set to direct in November 2022, with Michael Starrbury rewriting the script. Filming was delayed due to the 2023 Writers Guild of America strike, and the film is expected to be released on November 7, 2025.

Avengers 5 (2026)

Marvel Studios announced Avengers: The Kang Dynasty at San Diego Comic-Con in July 2022, with Destin Daniel Cretton as director. Jeff Loveness was revealed to be writing the screenplay. Cretton exited in November 2023, and Michael Waldron was hired as writer. Jonathan Majors was originally set to reprise his role as Kang the Conqueror in The Kang Dynasty, but was fired in December 2023. Filming was originally expected to begin in early 2024, but was delayed and Majors's firing. Avengers 5 is scheduled to release on May 1, 2026.

Avengers: Secret Wars (2027)

Marvel Studios announced Avengers: Secret Wars at San Diego Comic-Con in July 2022, with Waldron writing the screenplay. Majors was set to reprise his role as Kang until his firing in December 2023, and the film is set to release on May 7, 2027
